ABOUT OWL

Owls are nocturnal birds of prey known for their distinctively large eyes and silent flight. They belong to the order Strigiformes and are found in many parts of the world, including North and South America, Europe, Asia, Africa, and Australia.

Here are some interesting facts about Owl:

  • Owls are birds of prey, which means they hunt other animals for food. They are known for their silent flight and sharp talons.
  • There are around 250 different species of owls in the world, and they are found on every continent except for Antarctica.
  • Owls have unique physical adaptations that make them excellent hunters. For example, they have asymmetrical ears that allow them to pinpoint the location of their prey by sound alone.
  • Owls are mostly nocturnal, meaning they are active at night. However, some species, such as the snowy owl, are active during the day as well.
